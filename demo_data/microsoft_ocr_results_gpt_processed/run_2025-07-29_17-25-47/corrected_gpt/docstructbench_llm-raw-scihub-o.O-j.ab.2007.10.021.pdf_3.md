In summary, we describe here a protocol of modified
PCR-based site-directed mutagenesis used to splice together different regions of a gene in a one-tube PCR. In
comparison with methods now available for assembling
DNA segments of a gene in tandem, it is simple, convenient, and time-saving. As no nearby restriction sites are required or engineered into the mutagenic primer, a deletion
can be introduced anywhere within the sequence of interest. If more than three DNA segments of a gene are to
be assembled in tandem, one more round of PCR mutagenesis with the desired mutagenic primer should be performed
using the mutant plasmid DNA obtained from the last
round of PCR mutagenesis as template. We routinely use
this procedure in our laboratory with great success.
