# Mean G-curves

Estimating mean $G \left( r \right)$ as ratio of means as in Baddeley et al.
(1993) and using the variance estimator for ratio estimators

leads to the mean curves and confidence bands shown in
Fig. 11. Figure 12 shows that the assumption of a linear relation
between numerator and denominator in the reduced sample
estimator $\widehat { \mathrm { G } } _ { \mathrm { r } }$ is realistic. The EM algorithm yielded overall
mean and variances from the ratio estimators. Figure 13 also
shows the overall sample mean with confidence bands esti-
mated from the sample variance.
